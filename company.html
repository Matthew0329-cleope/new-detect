About the Cancer Research Institute

The Cancer Research Institute (CRI) is a non-profit organization dedicated to advancing cancer immunotherapy research. Founded on the belief that the body's immune system holds the key to defeating cancer, CRI funds cutting-edge research and clinical trials aimed at developing new and more effective immune-based therapies.

Our Mission

CRI's mission is to conquer cancer by fueling the discovery and development of powerful immunotherapies. They achieve this through:

  • Funding Research: Supporting innovative research projects led by scientists at top academic institutions and medical centers around the world.
  • Clinical Trials Support: Providing resources and expertise to advance promising immunotherapeutic approaches into clinical trials.
  • Education and Outreach: Educating the public and the medical community about the potential of cancer immunotherapy.
  • Convening Experts: Bringing together leading researchers to foster collaboration and accelerate progress.

Pioneering AI in Cancer Research

The Cancer Research Institute recognizes the transformative potential of artificial intelligence (AI) to accelerate cancer research and improve patient outcomes. While CRI's primary focus is on immunotherapy, they support and are involved in projects that utilize AI in several key areas:

  1. Identifying New Targets: AI can analyze vast amounts of genomic and molecular data to pinpoint new targets for immunotherapeutic intervention. This helps researchers discover novel ways to activate the immune system against cancer.
  2. Predicting Patient Response: Machine learning algorithms can be trained to predict which patients are most likely to respond to specific immunotherapies. This enables doctors to personalize treatment strategies and improve effectiveness.
  3. Accelerating Drug Discovery: AI can speed up the process of developing new immunotherapeutic drugs by analyzing complex biological data and predicting the efficacy of potential drug candidates.
  4. Improving Clinical Trials: AI can help design more efficient and effective clinical trials by optimizing patient selection, monitoring treatment response, and identifying potential biomarkers.
